# Weeks 9-12 — Ongoing Link Acquisition (User Executes)

By week 9, the easy wins are spent. Directory listings are submitted. Reviews
are accumulating. The next phase is harder, slower, and more compounding:
**editorial backlinks from real publications and resource pages**.

This is where competitors with 200+ referring domains earned their lead.
You catch up by doing this work consistently, not by doing it in a single
sprint.

**Time required**: 3-5 hours per week, indefinitely.

---

## Strategy 1 — HARO (Help A Reporter Out)

HARO connects journalists with sources. Sign up, get 3 emails per day with
journalist queries, reply when you have genuine expertise on the topic.
Real responses occasionally become backlinks in major publications.

### Signup
- URL: https://www.helpareporter.com/sources
- Free tier is sufficient
- Choose: Business / Finance, High Tech, Lifestyle categories

### Pitching rules
1. Reply within 1-2 hours of the email (queries close fast)
2. Lead with the value, not the credentials
3. Give a quotable 2-3 sentence answer that stands alone
4. Bio at the end: 1 line + the URL
5. Pitch 3-5 times per week — out of 50 pitches, maybe 2-5 land

### Bio for HARO replies
```
Muhammad Abdullah Chattha, Founder of Webcore Solutions, a Dubai digital
agency. Reach me at muhammad@webcoreuae.com or via
https://www.webcoreuae.com
```

### Sample response format
```
Hi [Journalist],

In response to your query on [topic]:

[Quotable 2-3 sentence answer — direct, opinionated, specific. Avoid
hedging. Mention a concrete example or data point if you have one.]

For context: [1 line — e.g., "Webcore Solutions has worked with 40+
UAE-based SaaS companies on this exact problem since 2019."]

Happy to expand if useful.

— Muhammad Abdullah Chattha
Founder, Webcore Solutions
https://www.webcoreuae.com
```

Track HARO replies in a simple spreadsheet:

| Date | Query topic | Publication | Replied | Used | URL |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Realistic outcome: 1-2 placements per quarter from HARO. Sounds low, but
each placement is a backlink from a Domain Rating 70+ publication —
worth ~50 directory backlinks each.

## Strategy 2 — Resource page link building

Resource pages list "best X for Y". A page titled "Top Dubai web
development agencies" linking to webcoreuae.com is worth a lot.

### Find the pages

Search Google for these query patterns:

```
"web development agencies" inurl:dubai
"top digital agencies" inurl:dubai
"best seo agencies uae"
"web design companies dubai" -site:webcoreuae.com
intitle:"resources" "dubai agency"
intitle:"recommended" "dubai web design"
```

Look for pages from:
- UAE business associations
- Industry blogs that have published listicles
- University or government resource pages (rare, gold when they happen)
- Local Chamber of Commerce member directories

### Email template — resource page outreach

Subject:
```
Possible addition to your "Top Dubai [X]" resource page
```

Body:
```
Hi [Editor first name],

I came across your "[Exact page title]" page — useful list, especially the
[mention one specific entry you found genuinely helpful, e.g., "the way you
broke down minimum project size for each agency"].

I wanted to flag Webcore Solutions for possible inclusion if you ever do a
refresh. We're a Dubai-headquartered agency, founded 2012, 25-person team,
covering web development, software, and SEO for UAE businesses.

Why we might fit the list:
- [Specific factor 1: e.g., "Fixed-scope pricing — no hourly billing surprises"]
- [Specific factor 2: e.g., "WCAG 2.2 accessibility built into every web project"]
- [Specific factor 3: e.g., "450+ client projects with publicly verifiable Clutch reviews"]

If the page is currently closed to additions, I completely understand. If
it's open, our company URL is https://www.webcoreuae.com — happy to send
project samples or specific data points if useful.

Best,
Muhammad Abdullah Chattha
```

## Strategy 3 — Broken link building

Find broken links on Dubai business / tech websites where the broken page
was about a topic we cover. Offer our equivalent page as a replacement.

### Find broken links

Use a free Chrome extension like "Check My Links" or paid tools (Ahrefs).
Manual flow:

1. Pick a Dubai tech blog or business publication
2. Open Check My Links on each page
3. Note any 404s pointing to external sites
4. For each 404, check the original target with web.archive.org — what
   was the page about?
5. If we have an equivalent or better resource, pitch it as a replacement

### Email template — broken link outreach

Subject:
```
Broken link on your [page title] page
```

Body:
```
Hi [name],

Quick note — I was reading your "[page title]" article (great breakdown
of [specific topic]) and noticed a broken link.

The link to [old URL or anchor text] is returning a 404. The original page
appears to have been removed from [old domain].

If you're looking for a replacement covering the same topic, we wrote
[our equivalent article title] last quarter:
https://www.webcoreuae.com/[blog-post-slug]

It covers [1 sentence of what the post does]. If it's useful as a
replacement, great. If not, no problem — just figured you'd want to know
about the 404 either way.

Best,
Muhammad Abdullah Chattha
Founder, Webcore Solutions
```

Realistic outcome: 1-3 placements per month. Lower volume than other
methods, but acceptance rate is higher because you're providing value
(flagging a 404).

## Strategy 4 — Competitor backlink analysis (Ahrefs free tools)

Find where competitors got backlinks. Pitch those same sites.

### Free tools
- Ahrefs free Backlink Checker: https://ahrefs.com/backlink-checker
  (limited to top 100 backlinks per domain, free)
- Moz Link Explorer free tier: 10 queries/month

### Workflow

1. Identify 5-10 competing UAE agencies — search "top dubai web development
   agencies" and pick the actual local agencies (skip global aggregators)
2. For each, run their domain through the free Ahrefs checker
3. Note any backlinks from publications, resource pages, or industry blogs
4. Skip: low-quality directories (already covered in weeks 2-3),
   competitor case studies (not a real link to ask for), forum profiles
5. Pitch the publications and resource pages using templates from Strategy 1-3

This is repeat work, not a one-time task. Plan 1-2 hours per week running
fresh competitor analyses and pitching newly-discovered sources.

---

## What "success" looks like by end of week 12

- 30-50 indexed backlinks across all sources
- 5-10 from real publications (not directories)
- HARO process running with 1-2 placements achieved
- Steady cadence of 1-2 outreach emails per business day
- First measurable ranking improvement on Google Search Console

You will NOT yet be ranking #1 for "Webcore Solutions". You may be ranking
top 5 for "Webcore Solutions UAE", "Webcore Solutions Dubai", and several
long-tail queries from the blog posts in week 4. That is the right pace.

---

## After week 12

Link building does not stop at week 12. It continues forever, at lower
intensity. Set a rhythm:

- **Monday**: 30 min HARO queries
- **Wednesday**: 1 hour resource page outreach (5-10 emails)
- **Friday**: 30 min broken link / competitor analysis

Three hours per week of consistent outreach, sustained for 12-18 months,
is what closes the ranking gap with established competitors.

There is no shortcut. SEO is the closest thing in business to compound
interest — boring while it works, magic if you don't quit.
