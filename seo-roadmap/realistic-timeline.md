## What is NOT promised here

A few outcomes are sometimes promised in SEO sales pitches but are NOT
in this plan because they cannot be honestly predicted:

- ❌ "#1 for Webcore Solutions in 90 days" — competing with companies that
   have 10-year head starts on backlinks. Not happening in 90 days.
- ❌ "Featured snippets on competitive queries" — these are awarded based
   on a mix of authority + content quality + freshness. Authority is the
   missing input for the first 6-12 months.
- ❌ "10x organic traffic in 6 months" — possible only if starting from
   near-zero traffic. We probably have moderate baseline. 2-3x in 6 months
   is realistic for the trajectory above.
- ❌ "1000+ keywords ranked" — vanity metric. 50 well-chosen rankings on
   commercial-intent queries is more valuable.

## What success looks like if you sustain the plan

By month 12-15, assuming consistent execution:

- 150-220 indexed referring domains
- DR 25-35
- 30-50 blog posts published
- 15-25 reviews across Google / Clutch / Trustpilot
- 3-6 published guest posts / editorial mentions
- 5-10 HARO placements in mid-tier publications
- Top 3 ranking for "Webcore Solutions UAE", "Webcore Solutions Dubai",
  "Dubai SEO agency Webcore"
- Mid-page-1 ranking for "Dubai web development agency",
  "UAE software development company"
- 2,000-5,000 organic monthly visitors (depending on which blog topics
  break through)

This is conservative. It assumes 5-10 hours per week of consistent work.
If the user puts in more time, results compress slightly. If less, they
stretch.

## How to use this file

- Print it. Pin it. Update the row for "this week" every Friday.
- If a row's expected outcome did not happen, look at `measurement.md` →
  Red flags section.
- Do not skip ahead because you're impatient. Skipping week 2 directory
  submissions means weeks 5-6 PR pitches have nothing to point to.
- Compound interest is boring while it works. Trust the process.
